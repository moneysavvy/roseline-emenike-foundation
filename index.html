Our Mission

Building Bridges to Opportunity

The Roseline Emenike Foundation exists to transform disability inclusion in Nigeria, creating pathways to independence and dignity for children with disabilities.

Our Core Mission

To close the disability inclusion gap in Nigeria by expanding access to assistive technology and rehabilitation services, while investing in local leaders and organizations to foster sustainable, community-rooted change.

Our Partnership Model

In strategic partnership with the Emenike-Ogunnaike Foundation (EOF) in California, we bridge international support with grassroots action, ensuring that global resources create profound and lasting local impact.

Our Programs

Proven Solutions for Lasting Impact

We focus on four interconnected, evidence-based pillars that create measurable change in the lives of children and their communities.

Assistive Technology

Providing life-changing tools like wheelchairs, hearing aids, and communication devices to ensure no child is limited by a lack of resources.

Rehabilitation Services

Bringing physical, occupational, and speech therapy directly into communities to improve function and promote long-term independence.

Educational Inclusion

Working to increase school enrollment through accessibility improvements, teacher training, and inclusive community initiatives.

Ecosystem Strengthening

Supporting local disability organizations with strategic grants and capacity building to scale their impact and create sustainable change.

Stories of Hope

Lives Transformed by Your Support

Behind every number is a human story. These are the real-world impacts of our collective work, made possible by partners and donors like you.

Amina's Journey to the Classroom

At 8 years old, Amina received her first wheelchair. It wasn't just a device; it was a key. Today, she attends school, plays with friends, and dreams of becoming a teacher, proving that mobility is the first step to possibility.

— Community Partner, Kano State

The Power of a Voice

Through our rehabilitation services, 12-year-old Chidi learned to communicate using assistive technology. His school in Lagos, once unequipped, now has three other children with hearing impairments enrolled, inspired by his success.

— Education Partner, Lagos
